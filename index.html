las Muñecas

Origins and Background

La Isla de las Muñecas meaning the island of the dolls. The dolls have called this island home since 1950, when a little girl fell into the water from a chinampa which is an artificial island created on fresh water for agricultural purposes.

A local named Don Julián Santana tried to save her and leapt in, but the infant unfortunately drowned. To please the infant girl spirit, Don Julián set up a cross and started hanging dolls.

Things to note when going 

When going to the La Isla de las Muñecas you must be careful and of the following, if not there may be dire consequences... 

Location

The Island of Dolls, also known as Isla de las Muñecas, is located near Mexico City in the Xochimilco canals.

Accessibility

The best way to get to the Island of the Dolls is by taking a tour from one of the many operators that offer tours to Xochimilco.

Timing

It's best to visit the Island of Dolls during daylight hours, as the atmosphere can become quite eerie and unsettling at night.

 

Cultural Sensitivity

Be mindful of the local culture and customs, and respect the beliefs of those who may view the island as a sacred place.

Safety

Be aware of the slippery and uneven terrain on the island, and take care when walking around the canals.

Respectful Behavior

Remember to be respectful of the island's history and the memory of the young girl who drowned.

Mysterious legends and supernatural beliefs surrounding the Island

The island is said to be haunted by the spirit of a young girl who drowned there and the dolls are believed to be possessed by her ghost.

The condition and appearance of the dolls

The dolls that can be seen on the Island of Dolls are often old, torn, and dismembered, adding to the eerie and creepy atmosphere of the place.
